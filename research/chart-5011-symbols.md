# UKHO NP5011 Chart 5011 — Navigational Chart Symbols
## Day Skipper Relevant Symbols Reference

> This file covers chart symbols a Day Skipper student needs to recognise and interpret.
> Symbol letter codes (e.g., K11, P1) refer to sections in NP5011 Chart 5011 (INT 1 format).
> Where symbol codes could not be verified from accessible source text, they are marked **[VERIFY AGAINST NP5011]**.
> NP5011 8th Edition 2020 is the current version; a 9th Edition was advertised for 2026.
> The document is organised in lettered sections: A–B (general), C–F (topography), H–N (hydrography), P–U (navigation aids and services).

## Depths and Soundings (Section I)

### Datum
All depths on British Admiralty charts are measured from **Chart Datum (CD)**, which approximates to Lowest Astronomical Tide (LAT). Depths represent the least (shallowest) depth at any given location, ensuring a safety-first approach for surface craft.

### Sounding figures
- Sounding figures are printed in metres and decimetres on metric charts (e.g., **5₄** = 5.4 m).
- The subscript smaller digit represents tenths of a metre; no decimal point is used.
- On older fathom charts the unit is stated in the title block.

### Drying heights
- Areas that dry (cover and uncover with the tide) are shown in **green** on colour charts.
- Drying heights are shown as **underlined figures** (e.g., _1₂_ = 1.2 m above Chart Datum).
- These represent the height of the feature above LAT/Chart Datum, not below it.

### Depth contour lines (isobaths)
- Contour lines connect points of equal depth.
- Standard depth contours: 2 m, 5 m, 10 m, 20 m, 30 m, 50 m, 100 m, 200 m (deeper beyond).
- The 5 m and 10 m contours are particularly important for Day Skipper coastal navigation.
- Colour shading: dark blue = 0–2 m, medium blue = 2–5 m, light blue = 5–10 m, white = >10 m (varies by chart).

### Depth qualifiers [VERIFY AGAINST NP5011 for exact codes]
- **ED** — Existence Doubtful (the feature may not exist)
- **SD** — Sounding Doubtful (depth reading uncertain)
- **Rep** — Reported but not surveyed/confirmed
- **Unexam** — Area not examined by hydrographic survey
- **PA** — Position Approximate (location imprecise)

### Dredged channels
- A dredged channel is shown with a dashed outline and labelled with maintained depth (e.g., "7.0m" or "Maintained depth 13.5m"). [VERIFY AGAINST NP5011 for exact I-section codes]

## Rocks and Underwater Hazards (Section K)

> **Safety-critical section.** Students must distinguish between rocks that are always dangerous (submerged), rocks that cover and uncover (drying), and rocks at the surface (awash). Confusion between these can result in grounding.

### Rock symbols — the key distinctions

| Symbol (visual) | NP5011 Code | Description | Safety implication |
|----------------|-------------|-------------|-------------------|
| Asterisk `*` alone | K11 | Rock that **covers and uncovers** (drying rock); height above CD unknown | Danger at low water; may be visible at low tide |
| Asterisk `*` with underlined height | K11 variant | Drying rock with known height above CD (underlined figure alongside) | Known drying height; clear at high water |
| Plus sign with 4 dots `+` (circled) | K12 | Rock **awash at Chart Datum** (at the waterline at lowest tide) | Always at or very near surface — extreme danger |
| Cross/plus `+` with dotted surround | K13 | **Submerged rock, depth unknown** — dangerous to navigation | Always underwater but depth unknown — treat as dangerous |
| Sounding with dotted surround | K14 [VERIFY] | Submerged rock with known depth | Depth given; assess clearance |

**Notes:**
- K11 and K12 codes align with INT 1 standard as confirmed by multiple sources including David Burch Navigation Blog (2016) and cross-reference with US Chart No. 1.
- The circled/dotted surrounds on K12 and K13 visually signal danger.
- A drying rock surrounded by the green drying-area colour indicates it uncovers at low water.

### Shoal and breakers
- **Shoal** — shallow water area, often with fine dotted boundary [VERIFY AGAINST NP5011]
- **Breakers** / **Br** — waves breaking over a shoal or submerged feature; shown as a symbol or the abbreviation **Br**

### Wrecks (K section)

| Description | Visual | Code | Meaning |
|-------------|--------|------|---------|
| Wreck, dangerous (mast showing) | Wreck symbol with dotted surround | K20 [VERIFY] | Wreck that is a danger to surface navigation; depth over wreck insufficient for safe passage |
| Wreck, dangerous (no mast) | Solid wreck hull outline with dots | K21 [VERIFY] | Dangerous wreck, depth over unknown or insufficient |
| Wreck, non-dangerous | Wreck symbol without dotted surround | K22 [VERIFY] | Safe to pass over with adequate underkeel clearance |
| Wreck, position approximate | Wreck symbol + **PA** | — | Location imprecise; give wide berth |
| Wreck, least depth known | Sounding alongside wreck symbol | — | Depth charted; assess clearance |
| Old/historical wreck, non-hazardous | Dotted symbol, may show as islet outline | K23 [VERIFY] | Buried or levelled — minimal hazard |

**Practical rule for Day Skipper:** Any wreck symbol surrounded by a dotted line (danger line) should be treated as a hazard. Do not attempt to pass over without confirmed depth information.

### Obstructions

- **Obstn** — abbreviation for obstruction (broken piling, submerged post, debris).
- Shown with dotted surrounding line if dangerous. [VERIFY AGAINST NP5011 for exact codes]
- **Foul** — foul ground (ground likely to foul anchors — old chain, debris, rock outcrops).
- **Foul** or **Foul ground** — labelled in text on the chart.

### Seabed nature abbreviations (Section J — relevant to anchoring)

| Abbreviation | Meaning |
|-------------|---------|
| S | Sand |
| M | Mud |
| Cy | Clay |

| St | Stones |

## Lights (Section P)

### Structure of a light description
A full light description on a chart follows the format:

**`Characteristic  Colour  Period  Elevation  Range`**

Example: **`Fl(3) R 10s 13m 5M`**

| Part | Example | Meaning |
|------|---------|---------|
| Characteristic | Fl(3) | Three-flash group, flashing |
| Colour | R | Red (omitted if white) |
| Period | 10s | Full cycle = 10 seconds |
| Elevation | 13m | Height of light above MHWS |
| Range | 5M | Nominal range in nautical miles |

**Note:** Buoy lights often omit elevation and range. Elevation is always above Mean High Water Springs (MHWS), not Chart Datum.

### Light characteristic abbreviations

| Abbreviation | Full name | Description |
|-------------|-----------|-------------|
| **F** | Fixed | Continuous, steady, unblinking light |
| **Fl** | Flashing | Single flash; dark period longer than light period; < 50 flashes/min |
| **Fl(2)** | Group flashing | Two flashes then eclipse; number in brackets = flashes per group |
| **LFl** | Long-flashing | Single flash of ≥ 2 seconds duration |
| **Oc** | Occulting | Light on longer than off; eclipse shorter than light period |
| **Oc(2)** | Group occulting | Two eclipses per group |
| **Iso** | Isophase | Equal light and dark periods |
| **Q** | Quick flashing | Continuous; 50–79 flashes per minute |
| **Q(3)** | Group quick | Group of 3 quick flashes; used on east cardinal marks |
| **Q(6)+LFl** | Group quick + long flash | 6 quick flashes followed by a long flash; south cardinal |
| **Q(9)** | Group quick | 9 quick flashes; west cardinal |
| **VQ** | Very quick flashing | Continuous; 80–159 flashes per minute |
| **VQ(3)** | Group very quick | 3 VQ flashes; east cardinal (deeper water, more exposed) |
| **VQ(6)+LFl** | Group very quick + long flash | South cardinal VQ variant |
| **VQ(9)** | Group very quick | 9 VQ flashes; west cardinal VQ variant |
| **UQ** | Ultra quick | ≥ 160 flashes per minute (rarely encountered in UK waters) |
| **Mo(A)** | Morse code A | Short-long flash (· –); used on safe water marks |
| **Al** | Alternating | Light alternates between two colours, e.g., Al WR, Al WG |
| **FFl** | Fixed and flashing | Fixed light with periodic stronger flash [VERIFY AGAINST NP5011] |

### Light colours

| Abbreviation | Colour |
|-------------|--------|
| (none) | White (default — colour omitted on charts) |
| R | Red |
| G | Green |
| Y | Yellow (or amber) |
| Or | Orange |
| Bu | Blue (rare) |
| Vi | Violet (rare) |

### Lighthouse symbols (Section P — structure codes)

| Code | Description |
|------|-------------|
| P1 | Major light / lighthouse (black star symbol with magenta flash circle/sector) |
| P2 [VERIFY] | Light on an offshore platform |
| P3 [VERIFY] | Lighted beacon tower |
| P4 [VERIFY] | Lighted beacon |
| P5 [VERIFY] | Light vessel / lightship |
| P6 [VERIFY] | LANBY (Large Automatic Navigational Buoy) |

**On charts:** Lighthouses appear as a black star with a magenta (pink-purple) flash symbol or sector line(s) extending from the position dot.

### Sector lights
- A sector light shows different colours (or light/dark) in different arcs.
- On the chart, magenta lines extend from the lighthouse position to show sector boundaries.
- A white sector typically indicates the safe approach direction; red or green sectors warn of danger or mark channel edges.
- The light description may list multiple characteristics, e.g., **`WRG`** (white–red–green sectors).
- Bearings of sector limits are sometimes charted; otherwise consult the Admiralty List of Lights (NP74–NP78 series).

### Direction lights
- A single narrow-beam light (usually white) marking a safe channel centreline.
- Shown as a single line from the light symbol. [VERIFY AGAINST NP5011 for exact codes]

### Light vessels and LANBYs
- A **Light Vessel** (lightship) is shown as a distinct oval hull symbol with a magenta flash circle and name/description.
- A **LANBY** is a large buoy used in place of a light vessel; shown as a large buoy symbol. [VERIFY AGAINST NP5011]

## Buoys and Beacons (Section Q)

> UK and European waters use **IALA Region A** buoyage. Region B (Americas, Japan, Philippines, Korea) reverses the lateral colours. All references below are Region A.

### How light characteristics appear on buoy descriptions
Buoy descriptions on charts use the same format as lights but typically omit elevation and range:
- Example on chart: **`Fl R 4s`** (flashing red, 4-second period)
- Slanted/italic text on chart = floating aid (buoy)
- Upright/roman text = fixed aid (beacon, lighthouse)

### Lateral marks (channel marks)

| Mark | Region A colour | Shape | Topmark | Light (if fitted) |
|------|----------------|-------|---------|-------------------|
| **Port hand** (leave to port entering) | Red | Can (flat-topped) | Red can/cylinder | Red, any rhythm |
| **Starboard hand** (leave to starboard entering) | Green | Conical (pointed) | Green cone, point up | Green, any rhythm |
| **Preferred channel to starboard** (main channel — treat as port hand) | Red with green horizontal band | Can | Red can | Fl(2+1) R |
| **Preferred channel to port** (main channel — treat as starboard) | Green with red horizontal band | Conical | Green cone | Fl(2+1) G |

**"Red Right Returning"** does NOT apply in Region A; it applies in Region B (Americas). In Region A, red is on your port (left) side when returning to harbour.

### Cardinal marks

> Cardinal marks indicate the direction of safe water relative to a hazard, named after the compass bearing of safe water FROM the mark.

| Mark | Colour | Topmark | Light characteristic | Mnemonic |
|------|--------|---------|---------------------|---------|
| **North cardinal** | Black over yellow | Two cones, both pointing UP | Q (continuous quick white) or VQ | Points up — north |
| **East cardinal** | Black top, yellow middle, black bottom | Two cones, base to base (◇) | Q(3) 10s or VQ(3) 5s (3 flashes — like 3 on clock = East) | E = 3 o'clock = 3 flashes |
| **South cardinal** | Yellow over black | Two cones, both pointing DOWN | Q(6)+LFl 15s or VQ(6)+LFl 10s | Points down — south; 6 flashes like 6 on clock |
| **West cardinal** | Yellow top, black middle, yellow bottom | Two cones, point to point (X shape) | Q(9) 15s or VQ(9) 10s (9 flashes — like 9 on clock = West) | W = 9 o'clock = 9 flashes |

**All cardinal lights are white.** The number of quick flashes corresponds to the clock position of the cardinal point (N=continuous, E=3, S=6, W=9).

### Isolated danger marks

| Feature | Description |
|---------|-------------|
| Colour | Black with red horizontal band(s) |
| Shape | Pillar or spar |
| Topmark | Two black spheres (one above the other) |
| Light | Fl(2) W (two white flashes) |
| Meaning | Navigable water surrounds the mark, but there is a danger immediately around it |

### Safe water marks

| Feature | Description |
|---------|-------------|
| Colour | Red and white vertical stripes |
| Shape | Spherical, pillar, or spar |
| Topmark | Single red sphere |
| Light | Iso W, Oc W, LFl W 10s, or Mo(A) W |
| Meaning | Safe water all around (mid-channel, fairway, landfall) |

### Special marks

| Feature | Description |
|---------|-------------|
| Colour | Yellow |
| Shape | Any |
| Topmark | Yellow X (optional) |
| Light | Yellow (any characteristic not used for white lights) |
| Meaning | Non-navigational purpose: traffic separation, exercise areas, outfall pipes, aquaculture, spoil grounds, racing marks |

### New danger / emergency wreck marking buoys [VERIFY AGAINST NP5011 8th Ed]
- Introduced post-2016 for newly discovered dangers.
- Blue and yellow vertical stripes; yellow cross topmark.
- Light: Al Oc Bu/Y (alternating occulting blue and yellow).
- Placed in pairs, one on each side of the danger.

### Beacon symbols (Q section)
- **Beacon** — a fixed structure (not floating); shown in upright (roman) text.
- **Beacon tower** — a taller structure; shown with a flag-like symbol or dot with description.
- **Cardinal beacon** — beacon with black/yellow topmark cones, same colour/topmark rules as cardinal buoys.
- **Lateral beacon** — red or green beacon marking a channel edge.
- Beacons in tidal areas on rocks or the seabed: shown as a circle with a dot, plus topmark symbol if applicable. [VERIFY AGAINST NP5011 for Q-section codes]

## Tidal Information (Section H)

### Tidal level abbreviations

| Abbreviation | Full name |
|-------------|-----------|
| CD | Chart Datum (≈ LAT) |
| LAT | Lowest Astronomical Tide |
| HAT | Highest Astronomical Tide |
| MLWS | Mean Low Water Springs |
| MHWS | Mean High Water Springs |
| MLWN | Mean Low Water Neaps |
| MHWN | Mean High Water Neaps |
| MSL | Mean Sea Level |

### Tidal diamonds

- A **tidal diamond** is a magenta diamond (◇) containing a letter reference (e.g., **◇A**, **◇B**, **◇C**).
- The letter corresponds to a table in the chart's title block or a nearby data box.
- The table gives, for each hour before and after High Water at a reference port (e.g., Dover), the **direction** (°T) and **rate** (knots) of the tidal stream at that position, for both spring and neap tides.
- Day Skipper students must be able to use this table to calculate tidal stream for passage planning.

**Code reference:** [VERIFY AGAINST NP5011 for exact H-section code numbers; H40/H41 referenced in some sources for tidal stream arrows]

### Tidal stream arrows

- Feathered arrows on the chart show the direction of the **flood** or **ebb** tidal stream at the time of strongest flow.
- Numbers alongside (e.g., **14,28**) indicate mean spring rate / mean neap rate in tenths of a knot (1.4 kn / 2.8 kn).
- Flood stream arrows typically point toward the flood direction; ebb arrows point toward the ebb direction.
- Some charts annotate with **Sp** (springs) and **Np** (neaps) rates directly.

### Overfalls, tide rips, races

- **Overfalls** — shown by a specific symbol (a series of wavy/jagged lines) indicating turbulent water where tidal streams interact with the seabed. [VERIFY AGAINST NP5011 for exact H-section code]
- **Race** / **Eddies** — may be noted in text on the chart.

## Traffic and Navigation (Section M — Tracks and Routes; Section N — Areas and Limits)

### Traffic Separation Schemes (TSS)

Traffic Separation Schemes are established in busy shipping lanes under Rule 10 of the IRPCS. On charts they appear as:

| Feature | Appearance | Notes |
|---------|-----------|-------|
| Separation zone | Magenta tint or hatched area between lane arrows | Traffic must not navigate in the zone except in emergency or to cross |
| Traffic lane | Large directional arrows within the lane | Shows direction of traffic flow |
| Inshore traffic zone | Hatched or stippled area between the TSS and the coast | Small craft may use this zone |
| TSS boundary | Dashed or solid magenta line | |
| Precautionary area | Circular or irregular magenta outline | Area where vessels must navigate with caution |

[VERIFY AGAINST NP5011 for exact M-section codes, e.g. M1–M10]

### Recommended tracks and routes

- A **recommended track** is shown as a dashed line with or without a bearing.
- A **deep water route** or **two-way route** may be shown with double parallel lines.
- Used in areas where shoaling or obstructions restrict safe navigation to a defined path. [VERIFY AGAINST NP5011 for exact M-section codes]

### Leading lines and clearing lines
- **Leading line** — two marks in transit; shown as a line on the chart leading to safe water.
- **Clearing line** — a bearing line drawn to keep a vessel clear of a hazard; may be noted by the navigator on the chart but is not a charted symbol per se.

## Anchorage and Prohibited Areas (Section N)

### Anchorage symbol
- **Anchor symbol** (⚓) — marks a recommended or designated anchorage.
- May be enclosed in a circle or have a dashed circle outline for a defined anchorage area.
- **N10** [VERIFY AGAINST NP5011] — anchorage area (designated).

### Anchoring prohibited
- **Anchor symbol with a diagonal cross or prohibition circle** — anchoring is prohibited in this area (often over submarine cables or pipelines).
- Labelled "Anchoring Prohibited" or similar text on chart.

### Prohibited and restricted areas

| Symbol | Meaning |
|--------|---------|
| Dashed line with hatching or magenta tint | Area with navigational restrictions |
| "Entry Prohibited" text + dashed magenta line | No-entry zone |
| Circle or irregular boundary with restriction description | Restricted area (speed limit, no wake, nature reserve, etc.) |
| "Firing Practice Area" or similar text | Military exercise or practice area |
| "Danger Area" | Active or periodic hazard area; check Notices to Mariners |

[VERIFY AGAINST NP5011 for N-section codes]

## Hazard and Restricted Area Symbols (Section N)

### Spoil grounds
- Dashed outline enclosing the text "Spoil Ground" (or historical variants: "Dumping Ground", "Spoil Area").
- Depths within spoil grounds are unreliable and change over time; soundings are not maintained.
- Day Skipper: treat as potentially shallow and avoid anchoring here.

### Cable areas (submarine cables and pipelines)

| Feature | Symbol |
|---------|--------|
| Submarine cable | Dashed line or solid line with "Cable" label, often in magenta |
| Submarine pipeline | Similar to cable but labelled "Pipeline" |
| Cable / pipeline area | Dashed boundary with text label |
| Anchoring prohibited over cable | Anchor + prohibition symbol over the cable line |

- **"Cable Area"** — may show as a dashed zone; anchoring and trawling are often prohibited.
- Important for Day Skipper anchoring decisions near shore.

### Military practice areas
- Shown with dashed boundary lines and text such as "Practice Area", "Firing Range", "Exercise Area".
- May have magenta tint.
- Active status is broadcast on VHF or published in Notices to Mariners.

## Land and Coastal Features (Sections C, D, E, F)

### Coastline types (Section C)

| Symbol | Meaning |
|--------|---------|
| Solid bold line | Definite coastline (accurately surveyed) |
| Dashed line | Approximate or unsurveyed coastline |
| Cliffs symbol | Rocky cliffs (a series of lines perpendicular to the coast) |
| Sandy shore | Fine dots or stippling along the coast |
| Mud/sand flat | Green stippled area drying at low water |
| Mangrove | Special mangrove symbol (relevant in tropical charts) |

### Colour coding on charts
- **Yellow/white** — land above MHWS
- **Green** — drying area (between LAT and MHWS — covers and uncovers)
- **Dark blue** — water 0–2 m (very shallow)
- **Medium blue** — 2–5 m
- **Light blue** — 5–10 m (or sometimes to 20 m; varies by chart scale and publisher)
- **White** — deeper water (typically >10 m)

| Symbol / Abbreviation | Feature |
|----------------------|---------|

| Tr | Tower |

| Radio Mast / Mast | Radio or television mast |
| Water Tr | Water tower |
| Windmill / Wind turbine | As labelled |
| Flagstaff / FS | Flagpole |
| Monument / Mon | Monument or memorial |

**Conspicuous features** (labelled "CONSPIC" or "conspic" in brackets) are reliably visible from seaward and useful for visual fixing.

### Lighthouse and light vessel symbols (Section P)

- **Lighthouse** — black star or sunburst symbol at the position; magenta lines/arc for light sector(s); name in roman (upright) type.
- **Light vessel (lightship)** — boat-shaped hull symbol with mast and light; labelled with name.

## Fog Signals (Section R)

| Abbreviation | Type |
|-------------|------|

| Whis | Whistle (wave-activated) |
| Gong | Gong |
| Siren | Siren |

A fog signal is shown with its characteristic on the chart: e.g., **Horn(2) 30s** = horn giving 2 blasts every 30 seconds.
