# IALA Maritime Buoyage System — Region A
## Day Skipper Reference Copy

> **IALA Region A applies to:** Europe, Africa, Australia, New Zealand, parts of Asia (including India, China).
> **IALA Region B applies to:** Americas, Japan, South Korea, Philippines.
> This knowledge base is built for **Region A only. Red = Port. Green = Starboard.**
>
> **Critical Rule:** In Region A, red marks are kept to port (left) when entering harbour or going upstream. In Region B this is reversed.

---

## Lateral Marks (Region A)

### Port-hand Lateral Mark
*(Keep to PORT — your left — when entering harbour / going upstream)*

- **Colour:** Red
- **Shape:** Can (cylindrical), pillar, or spar
- **Topmark:** Single red can (cylindrical)
- **Light colour:** Red
- **Light rhythm:** Any rhythm **except** composite group flashing Fl(2+1) — e.g. Fl R, Fl(2) R, Oc R, Q R
- **Retroreflector:** Red band or square

### Starboard-hand Lateral Mark
*(Keep to STARBOARD — your right — when entering harbour / going upstream)*

- **Colour:** Green
- **Shape:** Conical (nun), pillar, or spar
- **Topmark:** Single green cone, point upward
- **Light colour:** Green
- **Light rhythm:** Any rhythm **except** composite group flashing Fl(2+1) — e.g. Fl G, Fl(2) G, Oc G, Q G
- **Retroreflector:** Green triangle

### Preferred Channel Marks (Junction / Bifurcation Marks)

Used where a channel divides. The topmark colour and the composite group flashing light Fl(2+1) indicate the **preferred** (principal) channel.

#### Preferred Channel to STARBOARD (secondary channel to port)
- **Colour:** Red with one broad green horizontal band
- **Shape:** Can, pillar, or spar
- **Topmark:** Single red can
- **Light:** **Fl(2+1) R** — composite group flashing red (two short flashes, pause, one flash)

#### Preferred Channel to PORT (secondary channel to starboard)
- **Colour:** Green with one broad red horizontal band
- **Shape:** Conical, pillar, or spar
- **Topmark:** Single green cone, point upward
- **Light:** **Fl(2+1) G** — composite group flashing green (two short flashes, pause, one flash)

> **Memory aid:** The topmark colour (and main body colour) always indicates the preferred channel side. The distinctive Fl(2+1) rhythm distinguishes these marks from plain lateral marks.

## Cardinal Marks

**Rule:** Pass on the named side — e.g. a North cardinal mark: pass to the **north** of it (deepest water lies to the north).

- **Body:** Black and yellow horizontal bands; pillar or spar shape
- **Light:** White — either **Q** (Quick: ~50–79 flashes/min) or **VQ** (Very Quick: ~80–159 flashes/min)
- **Topmark:** Two black cones — the cone tips point toward the black band(s)

> **Clock face memory aid:** Think of a clock. East = 3 flashes (3 o'clock), South = 6 flashes + long flash (6 o'clock), West = 9 flashes (9 o'clock), North = continuous (12 o'clock, uninterrupted).

> **Topmark memory aid:** "SWNE — South West No East" — South cones point down (away from black), North cones point up, West cones point inward (base to base), East cones point outward (point to point).

### North Cardinal Mark
*(Pass to the NORTH)*

- **Colour banding (top to bottom):** Black (top) — Yellow (bottom)
- **Topmark:** Two black cones, **both pointing upward** (tips up — toward black top band)
- **Light:** **Q** or **VQ** — continuous quick flashing white (uninterrupted)
- **Memory:** North = top = black; cones point UP

### South Cardinal Mark
*(Pass to the SOUTH)*

- **Colour banding (top to bottom):** Yellow (top) — Black (bottom)
- **Topmark:** Two black cones, **both pointing downward** (tips down — toward black bottom band)
- **Light:** **Q(6)+LFl** or **VQ(6)+LFl** — six quick flashes then one long flash, white
- **Memory:** South = bottom = black; cones point DOWN; 6 flashes = 6 o'clock on clock face; long flash distinguishes south from west

### East Cardinal Mark
*(Pass to the EAST)*

- **Colour banding (top to bottom):** Black (top) — Yellow (middle) — Black (bottom)
- **Topmark:** Two black cones, **points outward (away from each other)** — base to base arrangement
- **Light:** **Q(3)** or **VQ(3)** — three quick flashes, white
- **Memory:** East = 3 o'clock = 3 flashes; cones point OUT (East-West away)

### West Cardinal Mark
*(Pass to the WEST)*

- **Colour banding (top to bottom):** Yellow (top) — Black (middle) — Yellow (bottom)
- **Topmark:** Two black cones, **points inward (toward each other)** — point to point arrangement; forms a waist/hourglass shape
- **Light:** **Q(9)** or **VQ(9)** — nine quick flashes, white
- **Memory:** West = 9 o'clock = 9 flashes; cones point IN (like a West-pointing arrow or waist shape)

## Isolated Danger Mark

Placed over a danger with navigable water all around. **Do not approach.**

- **Colour:** Black with one or more broad red horizontal bands
- **Shape:** Pillar or spar
- **Topmark:** Two black spheres (one above the other) — topmark is **mandatory**
- **Light:** **Fl(2)** white — group flashing two, white; period not specified in source [VERIFY AGAINST IALA O-1 for exact period]

---

## Safe Water Mark

Marks safe, navigable water all around (e.g. landfall buoy, fairway buoy, mid-channel mark).

- **Colour:** Red and white **vertical** stripes
- **Shape:** Spherical (preferred), pillar, or spar with spherical topmark
- **Topmark:** Single red sphere
- **Light:** White — one of: **Iso** (isophase), **Oc** (occulting), **LFl 10s** (long flash every 10 seconds), or **Mo(A)** (Morse code letter A: short-long)
- **Memory:** Vertical stripes (not horizontal like cardinal/isolated danger); the only mark using Morse A or LFl 10s

---

## Special Mark

Not primarily for navigation — marks a special area or feature. Refer to chart or pilot for purpose.

- **Colour:** Yellow
- **Shape:** Any shape not conflicting with a navigational mark (often can, conical, or spar)
- **Topmark:** Yellow **X** (St Andrew's cross / saltire)
- **Light:** **Yellow** — any rhythm that does not conflict with white navigational lights; often Fl Y
- **Purpose examples:** ODAS (Ocean Data Acquisition System) buoys, cable/pipeline areas, recreation zones, military exercise areas, traffic separation schemes, renewable energy installations

---

## Emergency Wreck Marking Buoy

Deployed as a rapid response to mark a new, uncharted wreck until the danger is charted and permanent marking established.

- **Colour:** Blue and yellow vertical stripes (equal bands)
- **Shape:** Pillar or spar
- **Topmark:** Upright yellow cross (unique — distinguishes from all other marks)
- **Light:** **Al Oc Bu Y 3s** — alternating occulting blue and yellow, 3-second period
- **Note:** If multiple buoys are deployed for the same wreck, their lights are synchronised
- **Source note:** Light characteristic sourced from sailingissues.com only — [VERIFY AGAINST IALA O-1 for exact current specification]

## Light Characteristics — Terminology Reference

| Abbreviation | Name | Definition |
|---|---|---|
| F | Fixed | Steady continuous light; does not flash or occult |
| Fl | Flashing | Light ON shorter than OFF; fewer than 50 flashes per minute |
| LFl | Long Flash | A single flash of 2 seconds or more duration; used in safe water marks (LFl 10s) |
| Oc | Occulting | Light ON longer than OFF (darkness shorter than light) |
| Iso | Isophase | Equal duration of light and darkness |
| Q | Quick | 50–79 flashes per minute |
| VQ | Very Quick | 80–159 flashes per minute |
| UQ | Ultra Quick | 160 or more flashes per minute |
| Mo(A) | Morse Code A | Short flash then long flash — · — (used on safe water marks) |
| Al | Alternating | Light alternates between two colours (e.g. Al Oc Bu Y = alternating blue and yellow) |
| Fl(2+1) | Composite Group Flashing | Two flashes, pause, one flash — used exclusively on preferred channel (junction) marks |

> **Period:** The time in seconds for one complete light cycle, shown after the characteristic, e.g. "Fl G 5s" = green flash, 5-second period.

## Verification Flags

The following details could not be independently verified from a second HTML source and should be checked against the official IALA publication **IALA O-1 (The IALA Maritime Buoyage System)**:

1. **Emergency wreck marking buoy light:** Al Oc Bu Y 3s — period and exact abbreviation convention. Sourced from sailingissues.com only.
2. **Isolated danger mark light period:** Fl(2) confirmed but specific period (e.g. Fl(2) 5s) not stated in either source.
3. **Cardinal mark VQ vs Q timing:** The distinction between which period applies (e.g. Q(6)+LFl 15s vs VQ(6)+LFl 10s) is not specified in HTML sources — exact periods require IALA O-1.
