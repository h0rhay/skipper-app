## Notes on RYA Official Course Structure

- Course duration: minimum 40 hours study time plus exercises and two written exam papers
- The RYA course-finder page (rya.org.uk) groups topics into nine broad headings: Seamanship; Coastal navigation and pilotage; Chartwork; Electronic charts; Position fixing; Plotting a course to steer; Weather forecasting and meteorology; Tides; Collision regulations
- The 17-topic breakdown above is the detailed syllabus used consistently across all RYA-recognised training centres
- No official RYA syllabus document with numeric section codes was accessible via the public website as of 2026-03-15
- The RYA Day Skipper Shorebased Notes (published by RYA, available as book or eBook) is the official accompanying study text

## Safety-Critical Topics Summary

Topics flagged safety-critical: 4 (Safety), 5 (IRPCS), 7 (Charts), 9 (Compass), 10 (Chartwork), 11 (Tides), 12 (Visual aids / buoyage), 13 (Meteorology), 14 (Passage planning), 15 (Restricted visibility), 16 (Pilotage)

Topics with IALA regional variation (Region A vs Region B): 12 (Visual aids to navigation), 16 (Pilotage). This knowledge base is built for Region A (Europe) — red to port.
