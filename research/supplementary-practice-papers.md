## Exam Structure Reference

Source: SUP-011 (Skippers Online — Exam Guidance, accessed 2026-03-15)

The RYA Day Skipper Theory exam consists of **two papers**, each 90 minutes:

- **Paper 1 — General Questions** (theoretical knowledge: COLREGS, meteorology, seamanship, safety, navigation principles)
- **Paper 2 — Chartwork** (open-book practical exercises using RYA Training Almanac and RYA Training Charts 3 & 4)

Key topic areas assessed: fixes and estimated positions, course to steer calculations, tidal heights, tidal set/drift, Collision Regulations.

## Apparent Wind

Source: SUP-009

Q: What is apparent wind?
(a) The wind speed measured at the masthead
(b) The combination of true wind and the vessel's own speed through the air
(c) The wind direction shown on a weather chart
(d) The wind felt only when the vessel is stationary
Answer: (b) — Apparent wind is the vector sum of true wind and the wind created by the vessel's motion.
Source: SUP-009

## Navigation Marks — Cardinal Marks

Source: SUP-009

Q: A buoy has black and yellow horizontal bands with two cones pointing away from each other (bases together). What type of mark is this?
(a) North cardinal mark
(b) South cardinal mark
(c) East cardinal mark
(d) West cardinal mark
Answer: (c) — An East cardinal mark has two cones base-to-base (pointing away from each other); its topmarks distinguish it from other cardinal marks.

## COLREGS — Stand-on Vessel (Sailing)

Source: SUP-009

Q: Two sailing vessels are on a collision course. Vessel A is on port tack; Vessel B is on starboard tack. Which vessel must give way?
(a) Vessel B, because it is on starboard tack
(b) Vessel A, because it is on port tack
(c) The vessel with the wind on its port side stands on
(d) The faster vessel gives way
Answer: (b) — Under Rule 12 IRPCS, a vessel on port tack gives way to a vessel on starboard tack. Vessel B (starboard tack) is the stand-on vessel.
Source: SUP-009

## Compass — Variation

Source: SUP-009

Q: What does the notation in the compass rose on an Admiralty chart refer to when it states a specific degree figure alongside a year and annual change?
(a) Deviation — the error caused by the vessel's own magnetic field
(b) Variation — the difference between magnetic north and geographic (true) north
(c) Compass error — the total of all magnetic errors combined
(d) Declination — the difference between grid north and true north
Answer: (b) — Variation is the angular difference between magnetic north and true north at a given location; it changes annually and is printed on the chart compass rose.
Source: SUP-009

(a) 6 metres
(b) 12 metres

## Buoyage and Pilotage — Lee Shore

Source: SUP-010

Q: What does the term "lee shore" refer to?
(a) A shore that is protected from the wind
(b) A shore that is exposed to the wind and toward which the wind is blowing
(c) A shore covered in kelp or seaweed
(d) A shore used as a temporary anchorage
Answer: (b) — A lee shore is one toward which the wind is blowing; it presents a navigational hazard as the wind pushes a vessel onto it.
Source: SUP-010

## IRPCS — Meeting Head-On (Power)

Source: SUP-010

Q: Two power-driven vessels are meeting head-on. What action should each vessel take?
(a) The faster vessel gives way
(b) Both vessels alter course to starboard
(c) The vessel to port gives way
(d) Both vessels maintain course and speed
Answer: (b) — Under Rule 14 IRPCS, when two power-driven vessels meet head-on, both must alter course to starboard.

Source: SUP-010

Q: What is the primary purpose of a VHF radio on board a vessel?
(a) Entertainment and music playback
(b) Measuring water depth (echo sounding)
(c) Communication with other vessels and shore stations, including distress calls
(d) Autopilot control
Answer: (c) — VHF radio is the primary short-range communication system for vessel-to-vessel and vessel-to-shore contact, including distress (Mayday) calls on Channel 16.

## Meteorology — Weather Check Before Departure

Source: SUP-010

Q: What is the most reliable recommended method to obtain a detailed marine weather forecast before departure?
(a) Observing a weather vane or outdoor thermometer
(b) Asking another sailor at the marina
(c) Checking a dedicated marine forecast service (radio, Navtex, or official forecast website/app)
(d) Reading the previous day's newspaper
Answer: (c) — The RYA recommends checking a dedicated marine forecast source such as the Met Office inshore forecast, Navtex, or VHF weather bulletins before any passage.

## Safety — Engine Oil Pressure

Source: SUP-010

Q: While underway under engine, you notice a sudden drop in oil pressure. What is the correct immediate action?
(a) Continue monitoring and increase engine speed
(b) Reduce speed but keep running
(c) Shut the engine down immediately and investigate
(d) Switch to the spare fuel tank
Answer: (c) — A sudden drop in oil pressure indicates potential engine failure risk; the engine should be shut down immediately to prevent serious damage.
Source: SUP-010

## Tides — Narrow Channel Navigation

Source: SUP-010

Q: When navigating a narrow channel with strong tidal currents, what is the recommended procedure?
(a) Increase engine speed to overpower the tide
(b) Maintain a safe speed, keep to the starboard side of the channel, and monitor channel markers closely
(c) Anchor and wait for slack water
(d) Cross the channel at right angles
Answer: (b) — Safe speed, correct side of channel, and attention to lateral marks are standard practice; crossing at right angles to the tidal stream minimises exposure time when crossing.
Source: SUP-010

## Day Shapes — COLREGS Reference Table

Source: SUP-012 (Nomad Sailing — Day Shapes Self Test, accessed 2026-03-15)

The following day shapes are assessed in the RYA Day Skipper exam (image-recognition format):

| Vessel Status | Day Shape |
|---------------|-----------|
| Restricted in Ability to Manoeuvre (RAM) | Ball–Diamond–Ball (vertical) |
| Engaged in Fishing (trawling) | Two cones apex-to-apex |
| Divers Down | Ball–Diamond–Ball + rigid replica of Code Flag A |
| Aground | Three balls (vertical) |
| At Anchor | One black ball |
| Constrained by Draught | One cylinder |
| Fishing with gear extending >150m | Two cones + cone point up |
| Mine-sweeping | Three balls (one at masthead, one at each end of fore-yard) |
| Sailing using engine (motorsailing) | One cone, apex downward |
| Not Under Command | Two balls (vertical) |
| Pilot on Duty | Pilot flag (Code H) |
| RAM with hazard to passing vessel | Ball–Diamond–Ball + two balls on obstruction side |
| Towing >200m | Diamond at masthead and at stern of tow |

Source: SUP-012
