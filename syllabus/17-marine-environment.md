# Marine Environment

## Summary
This topic covers the responsibilities of the skipper and crew to protect the marine environment: pollution prevention (oil, garbage, sewage), environmentally responsible anchoring, and an awareness of the international framework (MARPOL) governing vessel discharges. The Day Skipper syllabus expects a working knowledge of what is and is not permitted to discharge at sea under UK/international law.

## Key Facts

### Anchoring and Environmental Responsibility
- When selecting an anchorage, check chart symbols for seabed type — mud and sand cause less damage to the seabed than repeatedly anchoring on living reefs or protected habitats. [Source: SRC-001; transcript p.9]
- Allow adequate swinging room so that the anchor chain does not drag repeatedly across the seabed — this can damage benthic communities. [Source: SRC-001; transcript p.9]
- Prepare and handle anchor gear carefully to avoid fuel or oil spills from the windlass or engine during anchoring operations. [Source: SRC-001]

### Passage Planning and Environmental Awareness
- When planning a passage, consider how weather (wind, tidal stream) might cause a vessel to be set towards environmentally sensitive or protected areas. [Source: SRC-001; transcript p.36]
- Calmer conditions can be found in the lee of the land — choosing sheltered anchorages reduces the risk of dragging and ground damage. [Source: SRC-001; transcript p.36]

## Flashcard Candidates
- Q: What international convention governs pollution prevention from vessels? | A: MARPOL — the International Convention for the Prevention of Pollution from Ships | Source: Unverified — cite MARPOL text
- Q: Which MARPOL Annex covers garbage disposal from vessels? | A: Annex V | Source: Unverified — cite MARPOL Annex V text
- Q: May oily bilge water be pumped overboard? | A: No — it must not be discharged overboard; it must be retained and disposed of ashore or via a licensed waste facility | Source: Unverified — cite MARPOL Annex I
- Q: What environmental consideration should guide anchor site selection? | A: Avoid anchoring on sensitive or protected seabed habitats; prefer sand or mud over reefs | Source: SRC-001; transcript p.9
- Q: What is a holding tank used for? | A: Retaining sewage onboard for disposal in permitted areas or ashore, rather than discharging it directly into the sea | Source: Unverified — cite relevant domestic sewage regulations
