# Page 4 — Simple Sailing Theory and Points of Sailing

## Visible Headings
- SIMPLE SAILING THEORY
- POINTS OF SAILING

## Content

### SIMPLE SAILING THEORY

When you put a spoon into running water it is sucked into the flow.

Similarly, when air flows over a sail it creates a sideways force.

A combination of a sideways force from the sail and the water resistance pushes the boat forward like squeezing a bar of wet soap.

A sailing boat does not blow over as the force of the wind is counterbalanced by weight and buoyancy.

### POINTS OF SAILING

You can't sail closer than about 45° to the wind otherwise the sails flap and the boat slows down very quickly.

To make progress to windward (into the wind) you must travel in a series of zigzags, each at 45° to the wind.
