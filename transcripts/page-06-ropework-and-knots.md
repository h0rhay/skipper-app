## Content

### ROPEWORK

#### Making fast to a cleat
Rope led to 'open' side of cleat.

Follow with several figure of eights and one more round turn to increase friction.

#### Winching techniques
Using a winch gives more power for pulling in ropes.

Use the selftailer to grip the rope before you winch

Always wind the rope clockwise round the drum.

Keep the lead low to avoid a riding turn.

Turn the handle either way to pull in the rope.

When letting out gently ease rope round drum with palm of hand.

Letting fly — flip the rope uppermost. Care not to trap fingers between the rope and the winch.

[Jammer label:] Jammer — holds rope securely like a cleat.

When releasing a rope under load don't hold it close to the jammer — take the strain on a winch.

Add more turns for maximum friction.

### KNOTS

#### Rolling hitch
Used when you need a knot that won't slip when pulled at an angle. Ideal for taking the strain off another rope.

#### Round turn and two half hitches
Multipurpose knot. Can be untied under tension.

#### Reef knot
Mainly used for tying in reef points. Not very secure.

#### Sheet bend
Used for joining two ropes.
- single
- double

#### Clove hitch
Quick to tie and easy to adjust. Ideal for securing fenders.

#### Figure of eight
Easy to tie stopper knot — stops rope escaping.

#### Bowline
Makes a fixed eye in a rope which is very secure. Remains firm under tension. Many uses such as attaching jib sheets and for loops in mooring lines.
