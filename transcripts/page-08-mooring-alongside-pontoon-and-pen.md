# Page 8 — Mooring Alongside: Floating Pontoon and In a Pen

## Visible Headings
- MOORING ALONGSIDE
- ON A FLOATING PONTOON
- IN A PEN

## Content

### ON A FLOATING PONTOON

You should not have to adjust lines as the tide rises and falls.

You can use one long line for both tasks.

Labels on diagram:
- stern line
- stern or back spring
- finger pontoon
- bow or forespring
- float
- cleat
- bow line

### IN A PEN

Stern lines are attached to piles and the bow lines are attached first.
Usually the windward lines are attached first.

In some countries one of the piles is replaced by a pontoon for ease of access to the shore. The lines are adjusted to allow access from the steps or amidships. Pick-up lines are usually supplied on the piles to aid retrieval.

Labels on pen diagram:
- bow line
- ring rises and falls with tide
- pick up lines
- stern line

### Upper section — In a Pen diagram:
Top left: A top-down (plan view) illustration of a pen berth with two wooden piles on either side. Three fenders/buoys (white oval shapes) are shown hanging from the piles. A blue arrow points toward the pen entrance indicating the approach direction. The bow lines are attached to piles forward.

Top right: A top-down plan view of a yacht moored in a pen alongside a pontoon. The yacht is shown between two piles with lines running from the bow to the pontoon/shore and from the stern to the piles. Labels indicate:
- bow line (line from bow to pontoon at the shore end)
- stern line (line from stern to pile, upper right)
- pick up lines (lines hanging from the piles to aid retrieval)
- ring rises and falls with tide (label pointing to a ring fitting on the pile)

Caption: In some countries one of the piles is replaced by a pontoon for ease of access to the shore. The lines are adjusted to allow access from the steps or amidships. Pick-up lines are usually supplied on the piles to aid retrieval.

### Lower section — On a Floating Pontoon diagram:
A three-quarter perspective view of a motor cruiser/power boat moored alongside a floating pontoon. The pontoon is shown floating on the water (tan/sandy coloured). Multiple warps are shown rigged from the boat to cleats on the pontoon. Labels indicate:
- stern line (line from stern to pontoon, upper left)
- stern or back spring (diagonal line running forward from stern)
- finger pontoon (the finger of pontoon the boat is moored to)
- bow or forespring (diagonal line running aft from bow)
- float (the floating pontoon surface)
- cleat (fitting on pontoon)
- bow line (line from bow to pontoon, lower area)

Caption: You should not have to adjust lines as the tide rises and falls.

Caption: You can use one long line for both tasks.
