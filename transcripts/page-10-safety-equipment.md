## Content

### SAFETY EQUIPMENT

[Top half of page — cutaway illustration of a boat with labelled safety items]

Labels visible on the top half of the illustration:

- navigation lights
- anchor and chain
- fire blanket in galley — essential for throwing over the cooker
- softwood bungs for plugging broken skin fittings
- first aid kit
- icebox
- hand held VHF set
- almanac
- chartwork equipment
- up-to-date chart/pilot books etc.
- separate batteries for domestics and engines
- automatic extinguisher for engine space
- general spares, fuses etc.
- tools
- engine spares — propeller, fuel filters, belts etc.
- binoculars and hand bearing compass
- powerful searchlight or torch
- emergency VHF aerial
- fixed VHF set
- torch

Side note (top right):
Check RYA booklet Cruising Yacht Safety C8 for further advice on safety equipment and your National Maritime Authority for compulsory safety items required in your country.

[Bottom half of page — continuation of labelled illustration and separate distress flares diagram]

Labels visible on the bottom half of the illustration:

- smoke detector
- fire extinguisher at entrance to accommodation space
- radar reflector
- harnasses for bad weather and when going forward on deck at night
- floating rescue line for throwing to a person in the water
- bilge pump
- horseshoe lifebelt, drogue and floating light
- tender and outboard
- a lifejacket for each person on board
- extra warps can be used
- spare rope and cord
- liferaft

**Distress flares** (separate panel, bottom right):
- red parachute flares
- red hand flares
- orange pinpoint
- hand bilge pump — useful for bailing cockpit lockers
