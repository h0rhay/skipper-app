### PLOTTING YOUR POSITION

#### BY LATITUDE AND LONGITUDE

Lines of longitude run from pole to pole dividing the earth into segments rather like an orange.

Lines of latitude are obtained by projecting angles made from the centre of the earth to points on its surface.

#### Distance and speed

For all practical purposes a mile at sea is 1852 metres.
1' = 60' minutes of latitude
1' = 1 nautical mile
Speed is measured in knots.
A knot is one nautical mile per hour.

### BY DISTANCE AND BEARING

eg. 246°(T) from Skerries Point 1.6M

#### With a plotter

1  Dial in 246°(T)

2  Line up edge of plotter with Skerries Point.

3  Line up grid with lat and long on chart.

4  Draw line parallel to plotter in direction of bearing.

#### With parallel rules

1  Line up edge of parallel rule with centre of compass rose and 246°(T).

2  Keeping rules steady, walk them to line up with Skerries Point.

3  When aligned with Skerries Point draw line down edge of rules in direction of bearing.

#### Measuring distance

Measure 1.6M from Skerries Point along line of bearing.

Always measure distance at latitude-scale level with your position.

Never use longitude scale to measure distance.

Position 246°(T) Skerries Point 1.6M
