### TIDES

The gravitational pull of the moon and sun is the main cause of tides.

Moon and sun opposing – neap tides

[Diagram: Moon and sun on opposite sides of Earth — Neaps]

Moon and sun in line – spring tides

[Diagram: Moon and sun aligned — Springs]

Moon orbits the Earth over a 28 day cycle creating Spring and Neap tides

[Diagram: Moon phase cycle around Earth, labelled: Spring tides, Neap tide, Spring tide, Neap tide]

Difference between high and low water = tidal range

[Diagram showing smaller range (neaps) and bigger range (springs)]

### HOW MUCH WATER?

The depth of water under your boat is measured with an echo sounder – ultrasonic signals are transmitted to and reflected from the seabed to give the depth of water on a digital or analogue display.

The transducer is sited below the waterline – you can adjust for reading the display.

You can also calibrate for depth of water from waterline or bottom of keel.

Depth measured from transducer = 15.3m
Offset to waterline = 0.5m
Depth of water = 15.8m

[Display shows: METRES 15.3]
[Label: 0.5m offset]
[Label: transducer]

Chart shows depths you are likely to meet at the lowest predicted tide – Chart Datum (CD).

Height of tide is measured above CD.
Tide tables give the times and heights of high and low water for different ports.
