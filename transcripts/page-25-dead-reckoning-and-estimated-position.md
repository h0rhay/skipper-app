### DEAD RECKONING POSITION

It's possible to reckon your approximate position if you know:
– the course steered
– distance travelled (measured on log).

[Table:]
time   log    course
0900   10.2   070°(T)
1000   15.0   070°(T)

(4.8 miles travelled)

Dead reckoning position at 1000 (15.0)

The compass course is the boat's heading.
This would be 077°(M) here with variation of 7°W.

#### Effect of leeway

The effect of wind and tide means we don't always travel in the same direction as the compass course steered.

#### To plot an EP

1  Plot the water track from the 0900 DR position for the distance run (4.8 miles).
2  Apply leeway to give water track direction.
3  Add the tidal vector (set and drift for 1 hour).
4  Mark the estimated position.
