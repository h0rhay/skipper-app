# Page 28 — Waypoints and GPS Navigation

## Content

### WAYPOINTS
Waypoints (WPTs) are tools to help you navigate.
They are positions stored in the memory of a GPS.

For example you could use a WPT placed at the entrance of a harbour to help guide you safely into port.

You obtain WPTs from:
- the chart – double check you have the correct lat and long.
- publications such as almanacs, directories and magazines.

The GPS display can show the distance and bearing to a WPT and your current course and speed.
Be careful when you input a WPT into a GPS. It's as easy to put in the wrong position as it is to dial a wrong phone number.

When you input a WPT always check that the distance and bearing given by the GPS matches the distance and bearing that you have plotted on the chart. Any difference means you have probably input the WPT lat and long incorrectly.

Plot your WPT adjacent to rather than directly on hazards – you could hit them.

Never input a WPT straight from a book or magazine.
Always plot it on a chart to check your route.

In busy areas bear in mind that lots of boats could be using the same WPT.

### OTHER WAYS OF USING WAYPOINTS
You can plot your position quickly and simply by entering easily found positions as WPTs.
The GPS will give you a distance and bearing to the WPT and you can plot these to give a fix.
This is easier, quicker and less prone to error than plotting by lat and long but double check that you have entered the WPT correctly.

You can also use the WPT that you are travelling to.

Draw a web of distances and bearings from your WPT.

Compare the distance/bearing to your position.

Always pre-plan a course to steer to allow for tide – it's more efficient.

Plotting at speed
Conventional plotting can be difficult on a fast boat at speed.
Navigation must be pre-planned.

Be careful
Remember – GPS doesn't allow for tide.
- will sail a longer route
- could put the boat in danger.

It seems easy to just steer the bearing that the GPS gives you to a WPT but if there is significant cross tide:
- will sail a longer route
- could put the boat in danger.
