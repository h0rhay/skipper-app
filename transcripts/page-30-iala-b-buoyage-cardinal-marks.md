### CARDINAL MARKS COMMON TO IALA A AND B

Cardinals
Cardinal marks warn of danger, and remain constant throughout the IALA system.

[Central compass diagram with N, S, E, W cardinal marks:]

North Cardinal:
- Two cones pointing UP (both pointing upward)
- Black over yellow colouring
- Light: VQ or Q (continuous quick white flash)
- Example shown: "VQ(3)5s W Trouvée 978"

South Cardinal:
- Two cones pointing DOWN (both pointing downward)
- Yellow over black colouring
- Light: VQ(6)+LFl or Q(6)+LFl (9 white flashes)
- Example shown: "22 Q(9)15s W Trouvée Basse W" — 9 white flashes

East Cardinal:
- Two cones pointing AWAY from each other (base to base)
- Black, yellow, black banding
- Light: VQ(3) or Q(3) — 3 white flashes
- Example shown: "18 VQ(3)5s E-Trouvée 978"

West Cardinal:
- Two cones pointing TOWARD each other (point to point)
- Yellow, black, yellow banding
- Light: VQ(9) or Q(9) — 9 white flashes
- Example shown: "Q(9)+L Fl 15s Co S" — 6+1 long white, helps avoid confusion between S and W cardinals

[Note on diagram]: 6+1 long white flash helps avoid confusion between S and W cardinals.

Buoys are found in many shapes and sizes.
Solar panels and lights can make top marks difficult to distinguish.
Weed and guano can alter the appearance and colour.
