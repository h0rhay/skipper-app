### SECTOR LIGHTS
(A) In white sector
(B) In red sector

Iso.WR.(4)s 20m (10) 7M
[Breakdown:]
- 20m above MHWS
- equal light and dark — 4 secs
- white – visible 10 miles in good conditions
- red – visible 7 miles in good conditions

[Adjacent diagram labels:]
- on course
- too far to starboard
- too far to port

## Diagrams & Illustrations

### Sector lights diagram (bottom left)
Two illustrations of a lighthouse with coloured sectors:
- Left illustration: "In white sector" — showing white beam sector to port and starboard of safe channel
- Right illustration: "In red sector" — showing red beam sector indicating danger or off-course
Light characteristic notation: Iso.WR.(4)s 20m 10|7M
A real chart extract of Alderney (Channel Islands) showing sector light arcs. Labels: "ALDERNEY", "Blanchard Pt", various depth soundings visible (24, 27, 28, 36, 37, 38, 40, 41, 42, 44, 46, 57). Bearing lines A and B shown. Sectors marked with distances "10M" and "7M".

### Leading lights diagram (top centre-right)
Three panels showing a harbour entrance from seaward:
1. Lights misaligned — too far to starboard (upper light appears to left of lower)
2. Lights aligned vertically — "On course" (lights in perfect vertical alignment)
3. Lights misaligned — too far to port (upper light appears to right of lower)
Each panel shows two white circles representing the lights.

### Lighthouse diagram (top right)
Side elevation diagram of a lighthouse showing:
- Centre of light at top
- Height: 37m above MHWS datum
- MHWS (Mean High Water Springs) line marked
- "15s" indicating period
- "range 28 miles in good conditions" label
- Characteristic: Fl(4)15s37m28M

### Light characteristics reference (right side)
Visual comparison bars showing:
- FLG: Single flashing (short light periods, long dark periods)
- Fl(3): Group flashing (three flashes then darkness)
- F: Fixed (continuous light, no darkness)
- Oc: Occulting (more light than dark — short interruptions)
Each shown as alternating light/dark bars. "denotes colour" note alongside FLG.
