## Content

### PILOTAGE
Pilotage is the art of inshore navigation when you have visual references to help you find your way along the coast and in and out of harbour.
There may be lots of different hazards so good planning is essential.

Don't spend too much time down below – you put yourself in danger of where you are.
Making a good plan means you can navigate from on deck.

### PILOTAGE TECHNIQUES

Transits
[Two objects in line = transit. Keeps you on a specific track.]

Contours
You can work out where you are when you cross a contour and they can be followed in poor visibility.

Remember to allow for rise and fall of tide.
[GPS display shown: DEPTH 81.0] — "You can work out where you are when you cross a contour"

Back bearing
[Illustration showing bearing taken astern to a known point]

Clearing bearing
[Diagram showing a clearing line that keeps vessel clear of hazard]
"not less than 043°(M)"
"not less than 335°(M)"
You can go anywhere between the two bearings.

Bearing + distance
[Diagram showing position using bearing and distance from a mark]
"0.33°(T)"
"1.6M"
"1.5M"

IALA B buoyage [label visible in one diagram — annotation on chart extract]

Work this out in advance so you know when and where to expect the next buoy.

Turning points
Turn when chimney bears 320°(T).
"240°(T)" [label on diagram]
